The #1 rule of spring gear buying: Spring camping requires different gear than summer. You need a sleeping bag rated to at least 30°F, a sleeping pad with R-value 4+, and rain-ready layers. Don't buy summer-rated gear just because it's cheaper.

What to Look For in a Spring Tent

Skip the ultralight single-wall designs — they breathe poorly in damp spring conditions. Instead, look for:

• Full rainfly included (not an add-on)
• Mesh panels for ventilation (prevents morning condensation)
• Waterproof floor (16D or thicker)
• Sets up in 15 minutes or less (spring wind is cold)

🔥 Sleeping Bags — The Clearance Goldmine

Spring is the single best time to buy a sleeping bag. Here's why: manufacturers make bags for specific seasons, and a bag rated for 20°F or 30°F is winter inventory in April. Retailers want it gone. You want to buy it.

⚠️

Critical spring rule: Never buy a sleeping bag rated below 30°F for spring camping. Spring nights at most US campgrounds — including desert and low-elevation sites — regularly drop into the 30s. A 50°F "3-season" bag will leave you shivering.

What to Look For

• Temperature rating: 30°F or colder (gives you a safety margin)
• Synthetic fill (cheaper, handles damp spring conditions better than down)
• Full-length draft tube along the zipper
• Compression sack included

⏳ What to Wait On Until Summer

Spring isn't the right time for everything. Here's what to hold off on:

☀️ Summer-only Gear

If you're only doing summer camping trips, you can skip the spring-specific gear. Wait until June–July for the best summer deals.

🧥 Summer Clothing

T-shirts, shorts, and summer hiking clothes go on deep discount in late July and August. Wait.

🐛 Bug Spray & Sunscreen

These are warm-season staples. Check back Memorial Day for summer sales.

🛶 Kayaks & Water Sports

Peak water sports season = peak prices. Wait until August for end-of-season discounts.
