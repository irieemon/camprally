**Subject:** The 5 things I tell every new camper

Hey,

Day 2. Let's talk gear — specifically, the stuff that actually matters when you're just starting out.

Here's what I've learned after a few hundred nights under canvas:

**1. The sleeping bag is more important than the tent.**
A great sleeping bag in a mediocre tent beats a mediocre sleeping bag in a great tent every time. Temperature rating: always buy 10–15°F colder than you think you need. If you think it's going to be 40°F at night, bring a 20°F bag.

**2. Your sleeping pad is doing 80% of the work.**
Most of your body heat escapes downward, into the ground. A $40 sleeping pad with an R-value of 2–3 is the single best investment you can make.

**3. You don't need a $300 tent.**
The Coleman Sundome — often under $80 — has weather-tested better than tents triple its price. Spend your tent money on a better sleeping bag instead.

**4. The stove doesn't matter much.**
A $12 canister stove from Etekcity boils water in 3 minutes. A $150 Jetboil does it 30 seconds faster. Not worth the money, in my opinion.

**5. Start cheap and upgrade what breaks.**
Buy the $50 tent. Go camping 3 times. Then decide what you actually wish you had. Most people discover they overpacked, not underequipped.
